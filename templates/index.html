About CRAC Group

CRAC (Community Resilience against COVID-19) is a not for profit initiative focused on building the resilience of effected communities with the sole aim of ending the pandemic through;

  • Aggressive educational initiatives and altruistic projects
  • Sustainable strategic interventions designed to activate selfless giving aimed at supporting the efforts of government to control the spread and, ultimately end the pandemic.

The period and the challenges are one, which requires the Collaboration of, non-profit and for profit enterprises, social and professional groups, and individuals determined to ,provide support, transparently and every form of creativity, synonymous with the world today.

CRAC Group is motivated by the answer to the question, "can governments affected by the COVID- pandemic do it alone?"

The Community Resilience Against COVID-19 (CRAC) is powered by the collaborative effort of; Zebullon Developers Network, Host Mining Community Development Initiative (HMCDI), Krowdback, Secure ‘D’ Future (SDF), Lifeline Care Association, Glade Pay, Akin Sanda & Co, Sapphire Group and House 14 Hospitality.

CRAC donation strategy is based on the concept of Diiming designed and developed by Samuel Sobayo & Daniel Oratokhai. Diiming (Disposable Income Investment Model) is a funding system where disposable funds readily available to the lowest of the pyramid (N100 or 25 Cents) can be put aside daily by denying self of some un-necessary spend for the day into a locked investment or an interest.

Donations support from this efforts will be targeted to basic safety materials like mask and sanitizers to those who cannot afford one. After which we will direct support to care givers and health workers to ensure patients get the care they need and frontline workers get essential supplies and information.

The government cannot do this alone; we're all in this together.

WHAT IS DIIMING?

Diiming (Disposable Income Investment Model - DIIM) is a funding system developed by Samuel Olatunde Sobayo & Daniel Oratokhai, where disposable funds readily available to the lowest of the pyramid (N100 or 25 Cents) can be put aside daily or weekly by denying self of some non-essential wants for the day and diverting it to a locked investment or an interest or cause as in this case.

WHAT IS AN ESCROW?

An escrow is a financial arrangement where a third party holds and regulates payment of the funds required for two parties involved (in this case, CRAC & Nigerians) in a given transaction. It helps make transactions more secure by keeping the payment in a secure escrow account which is only released when all of the terms of an agreement are met as overseen by the escrow company.

WHAT IS THE PURPOSE OF THIS FUND?

The Community Resilience Against COVID-19 is a Resilience initiative that enables individuals, corporations, foundations, and other organizations around the country to directly support the work to help states prevent, detect, and respond to the COVID-19 pandemic. The Government cannot do it alone.

The funds would be used for;

- The production of masks to be distributed free and sold with the concept of purchase support (where for every one you buy, someone else gets one free) - Hand sanitizer distribution - Non-contact thermometer distribution - Care givers support - Educational campaign - Research support - Counseling support - Well-being
WHO CAN DONATE?

The Initiative is open to donations from individuals, corporations, foundations, or other organizations across the country.

For corporate, institutional, and foundation donations, please contact; donations@cracgroup.org

ARE DONATIONS TAX-DEDUCTIBLE?

Donations made directly to CRAC project account or through the website are tax-deductible (Nigeria Only) to the extent allowable by law.

For global donations, you can do that through the website or email, donations@cracgroup.org

WHERE DO THE FUNDS GO?

CRAC will receive donated funds, which will then be used transparently, according to the CRAC Strategic Plan.

As outlined in the plan, the vast majority of the financial resources needed will help the vulnerable and high risk communities, especially those with weak health systems, to shore up support for the families and the care givers in those communities.

Additional funds will help CRAC directly to fill in funding needs as it coordinates national response, as the pandemic evolves, resource needs will also change.

HOW WILL THE FUNDS BE USED?

The CRAC Funds would be used to support activities that are guided by the CRAC Strategic Plan, which organizes the response along three main pillars:

1. Healthy and safety masks supplies project 2. Care givers support project 3. Timely social health and well-being intervention projects
WHAT PERCENTAGE OF EACH DONATION WILL GO TO BENEFICIARIES?

100% of donations made through the website and all other channels of donations will go to Sun Trust Escrow account for the project. 95% of donations made will go towards the beneficiaries and for the implementation of CRAC Strategic Plan, with 5% used to cover the costs of online transactions, administrative & logistic cost.

DOES CRAC SEND MONEY DIRECTLY TO GOVERNMENTS (MINISTRIES OF HEALTH), OTHER NGOS OR BOTH?

CRAC will not send money directly to beneficiaries, but will use funds received from Nigerians and other donors to purchase material support and in the implementation of the CRAC Strategic plan.

HOW DO WE ENSURE FULL TRANSPARENCY?

Transactions reports would be published for the public to access, transparency and accountability are our guiding standard.

We have looked at creative ways to make sure everything is transparent;

1. Project account was secured by 2 of the NGOs in the group (we are open to adding more) 2. Signatories are from 4 organizations in the group 3. All funds are escrowed and monitored by the partner bank Sun Trust Bank 4. All disbursement will carried out by the partner bank (Sun Trust Bank) directly to vendors 5. Board will approve before any disbursement outside cost put aside for administrative expenses 6. All fees and administrative cost will be reported weekly 7. Details of all vendors will be made public (and we will be open to accept a better deal if any) 8. All donations and distributions will be analyzed real-time in the graph on the website for public viewing 9. We will be open to more creative ideas of distribution and amendment to needs area 10. We will carry all necessary government agencies along including the beneficiaries
